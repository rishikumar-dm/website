The Ranking Trap

This is the one thing that kept them stuck with the marketing agency for a year.

The ranking trap is basically this: the agency shows you that you are ranking for "best medspa for Botox in Flower Mound" — position 11, 12, 9, 10. Sounds great, right? The number of people searching for that keyword is pretty much nil. Nobody searches for that. Maybe 10 people a month or a year. And not even a single one is going to convert because you are already on position 10.

The Trap Exposed

You can rank position 1, 2, 3 for a keyword like "Botox side effects" or "after Botox how to take care of my body." Agencies show you these keywords and tell you that you are ranking and going to get clients. These keywords do help you rank faster. But they will not give you any patients or results.

The Actual Method

Finding the competitors is the number one thing. Then finding what they are ranking for using SpyFu, SEMrush, or Ahrefs — and finding the gap. Here is the key insight:

A competitor may have a page with only 10 visitors a month. But out of those 10, three may be buying a $1,000/month weight loss package subscription. You do not know that by looking at traffic alone. You find that by finding the highly competitive or high-cost keywords in Google AdWords and then checking how easily someone is ranking for those terms organically.

The Gap Formula

Find keywords that are non-competitive in organic search but highly competitive in pay-per-click — meaning people are paying $20 to $30 per click for them. That tells you advertisers are fighting for that keyword because it converts. But organically, there is little competition. That one gap alone can be $3,000 a month. Find 20 or 30 of those opportunities, and you win.
