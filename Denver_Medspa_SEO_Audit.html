Why medspa SEO stalls

Five medspa SEO mistakes that stall ranking and waste medspa traffic

Every Denver medspa above makes at least one of these — and so do most med spas. They're the heart of our medspa SEO audit.

Mistake 01

Mistaking brand traffic for local dominance

Rejuvenate's 20,922 visits look like #1 — until you see 90% land on the homepage for its own name and national “lip injections,” and only ~5% come from Denver searches. Brand volume books few local patients.

Mistake 02

Holding the brand term, abandoning the treatments

All three rank top-5 for “med spa denver,” then leave botox (best #6, then #23/#43) and dermal fillers (#6, then #73/#80) undefended. The brand term doesn't book the treatment.

Mistake 03

Leaning on the homepage and blog posts

Rejuvenate's traffic is 90% homepage; Vive leans on nano-brow blog posts. A local treatment search deserves a conversion-ready Denver treatment page — not a homepage or a blog explainer.

Mistake 04

Ignoring the easy, high-CPC money terms

“dermal fillers denver” (KD 6), “sculptra denver” (KD 6) and “laser hair removal denver” ($23 CPC) sit unclaimed or weakly held — trivially winnable searches worth real ad dollars.

Mistake 05

Vanity reporting

“20,922 visits” and “609 referring domains” get celebrated while calls, bookings, show rate, treatment value and collected revenue go untracked — and median link authority is actually zero.
